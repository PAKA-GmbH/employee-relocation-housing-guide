1. Market Reality Check

Is Nürnberg affordable compared to other German cities?

Yes — significantly. Nürnberg is a "B city" in German real estate terms: large enough to have real job opportunities, but considerably cheaper than Munich or Stuttgart.

City Average asking rent/m² Your budget's reach
Munich ~€24/m² Very difficult at these budgets
Stuttgart ~€15–19/m² Difficult; mostly outer districts
Nürnberg ~€12–15/m² Achievable, with effort

The key number to understand: Warmmiete vs. Kaltmiete

Almost everything in this guide uses Warmmiete — the total monthly cost including all building utilities (heating, water, building maintenance). When you see a listing, always clarify:

"Ist der Preis warm oder kalt?"

Kaltmiete is the base rent only. Nebenkosten (utilities/operating costs) add approximately €2.50–€4.00 per m² per month on top. So a listing at "€430 kalt" for a 25 m² apartment will actually cost you around €430 + (25 × €3) = €505 warm.

Always calculate total cost before comparing listings.

The official rent index (Mietenspiegel)

Nürnberg has an official Mietenspiegel (rent index) published by the city, last updated 2024. It shows average contract rents of €9.65/m² — but this reflects existing long-term tenants, not new market listings. New listings currently ask €11.85–€14.80/m² depending on the apartment size and district. The official index is mainly useful if you believe a landlord is charging above market and want legal leverage.

2. Your Two Scenarios — What to Expect

Scenario A: 1-room apartment, max €600 warm

Verdict: Challenging but real — requires speed and preparation.

Small 1-room apartments (20–30 m²) in outer districts cost approximately €450–€660 warm. At €600, you are right at the realistic lower boundary of the market.

What €600 warm buys you: - A studio or 1-Zimmer-Wohnung of roughly 20–30 m² - Typically outer districts: Langwasser, Steinbühl/Gibitzenhof, Schniegling, Röthenbach, Eibach - Older building stock (pre-1990, often pre-1977) - Basic finishes — may have no built-in kitchen (common in Germany) - Likely no balcony

Listings do exist: Immonet and IS24 regularly show apartments under €600 warm in Nürnberg. But they disappear quickly — often within 24–48 hours. You must have your documents ready to send before you search.

Supplementary paths to consider: - Subsidized housing (WBS): A Wohnberechtigungsschein opens access to apartments well below market rate. Apply at the Sozialamt (see Section 4). - Housing cooperatives: Join one now; waiting time is 6–24 months but the rent will be below €600 permanently.

Scenario B: 2-room apartment, max €1,000 warm

Verdict: Yes, this is achievable — good inventory exists.

A 2-Zimmer-Wohnung of 45–60 m² in Nürnberg's middle-ring districts ranges from approximately €750–€994 warm. Your budget of €1,000 gives you real room to maneuver.

What €1,000 warm buys you: - 45–60 m² in accessible districts (Gostenhof, Gartenstadt, Gibitzenhof, Langwasser) - Some listings even closer to the city center - Better building quality; balcony more likely - More leverage in pet negotiations (you are a higher-value, longer-term tenant) - More space for a future partner to join

Listings available: Immonet showed 481+ apartments under €1,000 warm in Nürnberg when last searched. This budget gives you genuine choice — take your time to compare rather than grabbing the first option.

Strategic recommendation: If your finances allow it, go for Scenario B from the start. A larger apartment solves the pet-friendliness question (more space = easier landlord conversation), eliminates any need to move later when your partner joins, and the €1,000 budget gives you negotiating room.

Wohnberechtigungsschein (WBS) — Social Housing Certificate

A WBS gives you access to state-subsidized apartments rented significantly below market rate. Even if you don't think you qualify, it's worth applying — the check is free.

Who qualifies: Anyone whose household net income does not exceed Bavarian income limits. As of 2024–2025, approximately €12,000–€14,000/year net for a single person (higher with additional household members). Check exactly with the office below.

Apply in Nürnberg at: - Sozialamt Nürnberg / Wohnungsvermittlung - Address: Marienstraße 6, 90402 Nürnberg - Phone: 0911/231-2195 - Email: wohnungsvermittlung@stadt.nuernberg.de - Hours: Monday–Friday, 08:30–12:30 - Website: https://www.nuernberg.de/internet/sozialamt_nbg/wohnungsvermittlung.html

Two types: 1. Vormerkbescheid: The city actively proposes a specific subsidized apartment to you 2. Allgemeiner WBS: You receive a certificate and apply independently to landlords offering WBS apartments

A WBS issued in Nürnberg is valid across all of Bavaria.

Tip: Apply for a WBS even if you plan to use the private market. It costs nothing and opens a significant additional pool of affordable apartments.

Housing Cooperatives (Wohnungsgenossenschaften)

Cooperatives offer: - Below-market rents (typically 10–20% below market) - Lifetime tenancy rights — you cannot be arbitrarily evicted or subject to rent hikes beyond legal limits - Full deposit refund when you leave (you buy shares upfront) - No Maklerprovision (agent fees)

The trade-off: you pay membership shares upfront and wait for a vacancy (6 months to 2 years typically). Apply now — the waiting time runs while you search on the open market.

6. Pet-Friendly Apartments — Your Rights & Strategy

The law is on your side

The German Federal Court of Justice (Bundesgerichtshof) ruled on 20 March 2013 (case VIII ZR 168/12): a blanket ban on dogs and cats written into standard rental contract clauses is legally invalid. Such a clause, buried in small print as a general term (AGB), is unenforceable.

What this means: - If your lease has a total pet ban only in the standard boilerplate, you can legally keep your pet - If the landlord wrote a specific, individually negotiated clause banning pets — that is more likely enforceable - Small animals (hamsters, guinea pigs, rabbits, fish, small birds) are considered normal residential use — no landlord permission is needed at all

However, landlords can still require that you ask for permission (Erlaubnisklausel) before keeping a dog or cat. And they can refuse if they have a valid, specific reason (overcrowding, documented problems). Refusal without cause is legally challengeable.

Bottom line: Do not hide your pet. Always disclose honestly, and use the strategies below to make your application irresistible.

What "Haustiere nach Absprache" means

This phrase on listings means "pets by arrangement/negotiation." It is a positive signal — the landlord is open rather than categorically opposed. Contact them promptly:

  1. Specify exactly what pet you have: species, breed (if dog), age, size/weight
  2. Emphasize: well-trained, vaccinated, indoor (if cat), quiet, no damage history
  3. Mention you have Haftpflichtversicherung (personal liability insurance) that covers pet damage — this is standard in Germany and reassures landlords significantly
  4. Offer to have explicit written permission included in the rental contract

Pets by type — practical expectations

Pet Legal right Typical landlord stance
Fish, small birds No permission needed Never an issue
Hamster, guinea pig, rabbit No permission needed Rarely raised
Indoor cat (neutered) Ask permission Usually grantable; say "indoor cat, vaccinated, neutered"
Small/medium dog (calm breed) Ask permission Easier with breed name + weight; mention parks nearby
Large or active dog Ask permission More resistance; compensate with more documents and reassurance
Exotic pets (reptiles, ferrets) Must ask Disclose honestly; some landlords resistant; be prepared to negotiate

Building your "pet dossier"

Prepare a mini-package to attach to your application or bring to the viewing: - Photo of your pet (clean, friendly, well-groomed — this genuinely helps) - Vaccination records (Impfausweis) - For dogs: Hundesteuerregistrierung (dog tax registration) if already in Germany; proof that the breed is not on Bavaria's Kampfhundliste (dangerous breeds list) - Reference letter from previous landlord confirming no pet-related damage - Your Haftpflichtversicherungsnachweis (liability insurance certificate) confirming it covers animal damage

Sample cover letter paragraph about your pet (in German)

Copy and personalize this for your application letters:

Ich möchte Sie gerne darauf hinweisen, dass ich [eine Katze / einen Hund der Rasse X] halte — [Name], [Alter] Jahre alt, [Rasse, Größe/Gewicht]. [Name] ist [kastriert und geimpft / stubenrein und ruhig]. Tierschäden in der Wohnung sind durch meine Privathaftpflichtversicherung abgedeckt. Ich kann auf Wunsch eine Referenz meines bisherigen Vermieters vorlegen, der bestätigt, dass [Name] nie Schäden verursacht hat. Ich würde die Tierhaltungserlaubnis gerne schriftlich im Mietvertrag festhalten.

Should you search specifically for "Haustiere erlaubt"?

Yes — use this filter on IS24 and Immowelt. But do not only look at listings marked as pet-friendly. Many listings say "Haustiere nach Absprache" or say nothing about pets at all. Private landlords on Kleinanzeigen.de often have no written policy and make individual decisions. A warm, transparent application with a pet dossier can convert a neutral listing into a pet-friendly one.

Important: A pet belonging to a different species or size than described in your application can be grounds for an Abmahnung (formal warning). Always describe your pet accurately.

7. Adding a Second Person Later

This is a very common situation in Germany and your rights are well-protected.

Can you add your partner to a 1-person apartment?

Yes. Under §553 BGB (German Civil Code), you have the right to request that your partner be allowed to move into your apartment. A life partner (Lebensgefährte) qualifies as a "legitimate interest" (berechtigtes Interesse) — the landlord must generally approve this request.

The landlord can only refuse if: - The apartment would become genuinely overcrowded (courts use a rough guide: 1 room + 9 m² minimum per additional person) - There are specific documented concerns about the incoming person

Practical process: 1. Send a written request (email is sufficient) to your landlord:

"Ich beabsichtige, ab [Datum] meine/n Lebensgefährt/in, [Name], in die Wohnung aufzunehmen. Ich bitte um Ihre Zustimmung gemäß §553 BGB." 2. Include your partner's full name, current address, and occupation 3. The landlord has a reasonable time to respond (typically 2–4 weeks) 4. If refused without valid grounds, contact the Mieterbund Nürnberg (see Section 14)

Anmeldung (address registration) for the second person

Your partner must register their new address at the Einwohnermeldeamt (residents' registration office) within 14 days of moving in. To do this, they need: - Passport or ID - A Wohnungsgeberbestätigung — a standard form signed by you (as primary tenant) or the landlord confirming the person lives at the address. Request this from your landlord promptly. - The Anmeldung appointment: book online at https://www.nuernberg.de (Bürgeramt / Einwohnermeldeamt)

Is your partner automatically added to the lease?

No. Being on the lease (as a Mitmieter/co-tenant) requires the landlord's agreement. You can request it — being a co-tenant gives your partner full independent tenant rights and protection. Even without being on the lease, however, your partner has the right to live with you and cannot be removed as long as you remain a tenant in good standing.

Which apartment size makes more sense?

If there is any reasonable chance your partner will join within 1–2 years, Scenario B (2-room, €1,000) is strongly recommended from the start. The reasons:

  1. You avoid the stress of moving again
  2. A 2-room apartment gives comfortable space for two people
  3. The landlord knows from the outset a potential second person may join — no surprises
  4. It is far easier to add a person to a 2-room lease than to argue for overcrowding exceptions in a 1-room

9. The Employer Confirmation Letter — How to Get One

What it is and why it matters

An Arbeitgeberbescheinigung (employer confirmation letter) is a signed letter on your employer's official letterhead that confirms your employment details. It is one of the most important documents for renting in Germany — especially if: - You have just started working and don't have 3 months of payslips yet - Your SCHUFA history is thin (as a newcomer) - You want to stand out as a reliable candidate

It is not something an HR company like PAKA GmbH provides — it must come from your direct employer (the company where you actually work). Ask your employer's HR department or your direct manager.

How to approach your employer

Most German employers are familiar with this request — it is standard practice. A simple message like this works:

"Hallo [HR/Manager name], ich bin gerade auf Wohnungssuche in Nürnberg und brauche eine Arbeitgeberbescheinigung für meinen Vermieter. Könnten Sie mir bitte ein kurzes Schreiben auf Firmenbriefkopf ausstellen, das meine Stelle, mein Anfangsdatum und mein Gehalt bestätigt? Ich füge eine Vorlage bei, falls das hilfreich ist."

In English:

"Hi [name], I'm currently looking for an apartment in Nuremberg and need an employer confirmation letter for landlords. Could you issue a brief letter on company letterhead confirming my position, start date, and salary? I've attached a template in case that helps."

This is a routine request — most HR departments can provide this within 1–3 business days.

11. Hidden Costs to Budget For

Upfront costs when moving in

Cost Amount Notes
Security deposit (Mietkaution) Up to 3 months Kaltmiete Legal maximum; paid upfront; returned at end of tenancy (minus any damage)
Kitchen (Küche) €0–€2,000 German apartments frequently come without a fitted kitchen. The previous tenant may sell theirs — check Kleinanzeigen.de
Moving costs €300–€1,500+ DIY with rented van, or professional movers
Apartment viewing trips Variable Travel to Nürnberg for in-person viewings
SCHUFA report €29.95 If using the paid instant version

Monthly costs beyond rent

Cost Typical amount Notes
Electricity (Strom) €30–€80/month Not included in Nebenkosten; sign your own contract (Stadtwerke Nürnberg or via Verivox.de)
Internet €25–€50/month Sign your own contract; Glasfaser/fiber is available in much of Nürnberg
Rundfunkbeitrag (GEZ) €18.36/month Mandatory household broadcasting fee; one payment per household
Contents insurance (Hausrat) €5–€15/month Strongly recommended; covers theft, water damage, fire
Personal liability insurance (Haftpflicht) €5–€10/month Essential; covers damage you cause to others — including pet damage
Dog tax (Hundesteuer) €75–€140/year Mandatory for dog owners in Nürnberg; register at the city

Annual Nebenkosten reconciliation

After your first full year, your landlord sends a Nebenkostenabrechnung (utility cost settlement). If actual costs were higher than your monthly advance payments (Vorauszahlung), you owe the difference. If lower, you get money back.

Typical gap: €50–€300/year either way. Budget for a potential extra payment and keep savings accordingly.

12. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Pitfall Risk Prevention
Hiding your pet Abmahnung (formal warning), eviction, or legal dispute Always disclose honestly — legal protection is on your side
Paying deposit before signing a contract Scam — money lost with no recourse Never pay anything without a signed Mietvertrag
Accepting verbal agreements Unenforceable in practice Insist on written contract for everything
Missing 14-day Anmeldung deadline Legal complications; delays permits and banking Register within 14 days at the Einwohnermeldeamt
Not getting Wohnungsgeberbestätigung Cannot complete Anmeldung Request this form from your landlord on day 1
Expecting to negotiate rent Rarely possible in tight markets Focus negotiation on terms (move-in date, deposit, pet permission) not price
Fake subletting (shadow sublet) Eviction if landlord finds out Verify the person offering the room is the main tenant AND has written landlord permission to sublet
Underestimating the kitchen situation Unexpected €500–€2,000 cost Ask explicitly: "Ist eine Einbauküche vorhanden?"
Assuming pets are automatically forbidden Missing valid options Check the law (Section 6), use the strategy, negotiate
Not budgeting for Kaution Cash shortfall at move-in Budget 3 months Kaltmiete upfront
